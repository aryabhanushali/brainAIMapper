Planning and Reasoning

Your prefrontal cortex is responsible for high-level reasoning, planning, and decision-making. It's what lets you imagine future actions, weigh consequences, and think strategically.

1. How Your Brain Plans

When you're deciding what to do:
  1. The prefrontal cortex activates when you face a goal (e.g., "plan a trip").
  2. It uses memory, prediction, and logic to simulate possible actions and consequences.
  3. You evaluate the best option and send it to motor areas for execution.

2. How AI Mimics Planning

LLMs like GPT-4 appear to reason by generating plausible next steps:
  1. You give it a prompt (e.g., "How should I study for finals?").
  2. The model predicts one token at a time ‚Äî drawing from patterns in its training data.
  3. The result is structured reasoning, like a step-by-step plan or argument.

4. Brain vs. AI: Planning

Prefrontal Cortex (Human) Large Language Model (AI)
Thinks through multi-step plans using logic and experience Generates multi-step text via token prediction and training patterns
Imagines future actions and consequences Samples likely next steps from its learned distribution
Uses episodic memory to inform judgment Uses training corpus to mimic expert reasoning
Updates plans dynamically as things change Can regenerate plans with new prompt/context

Just like your brain forms strategies, LLMs simulate planning by generating structured reasoning from past examples.
