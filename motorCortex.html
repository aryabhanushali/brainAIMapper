Motor Cortex: Movement & Control

The motor cortex sends signals to your muscles to control movement. It plans and initiates actions like reaching, walking, or typing.

1. How Your Brain Controls Movement

  1. Your motor cortex receives an intention (e.g., "grab the cup").
  2. It plans a sequence of muscle activations based on your body and environment.
  3. Signals travel down the spinal cord to contract muscles.
  4. Feedback from the senses is used to adjust movements in real-time.
