Cognitive Control

The prefrontal cortex enables you to focus, switch tasks, control impulses, and follow complex rules ‚Äî a set of abilities called cognitive control.

1. What Is Cognitive Control?

  • Task Switching: Shifting between different goals or mental sets (e.g., math vs writing).
  • Inhibition: Suppressing distractions and irrelevant actions.
  • Context Maintenance: Holding instructions or rules in working memory.
Cognitive control allows you to pursue long-term goals despite distractions.
